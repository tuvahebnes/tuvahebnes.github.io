TUVALU

a drowning nation 
Tuvalu's nine coral atolls are home to 
10 500 people. Firmly connected to 
family, culture and land.Until recently 
the richness of the soil and sea has 
provided a self-sufficient lifestyle for 
the families who share it.
            
But with an average elevation less than 
two meters above sealevel, this is a 
delicate environment. The accelerating 
effects of sealevel-rise are threatening 
the traditional ways of life.
